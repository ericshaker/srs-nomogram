Model

Multivariable logistic regression with stepwise selection (p<0.05). Four predictors retained out of ten candidates. Trained on 70% of the development cohort (n = 306).

Discrimination

0.77 / 0.861 / 0.802

Somers' D for training / internal validation (n = 144) / external validation (n = 721).

Interpretation

Estimates the probability that a neurosurgeon, in practice, recommends upfront resection for a given clinical profile — a measure of concordance with evidence-based selection criteria, not of benefit.

P(surgery) = 1 / (1 + exp(−η))

Model

Cox proportional hazards on the surgical subset (n = 222). Predicted survival from S(t | x) = S₀(t)exp(η), where S₀(t) are empirical baseline survival probabilities at 1, 3, and 5 years.

Discrimination

0.66–0.79

C-index range across 1-, 3-, and 5-year horizons (training 0.66/0.73/0.66, validation 0.79/0.76/0.71).

Note on reference

Predicted curves are anchored to the model's reference patient (continuous covariates at mean, indicators at zero). Setting all primary-site indicators to "No" reflects the phantom reference category, not a realistic patient — select one primary site for valid predictions.
